# Terrified Toys — Investor One-Pager

*Draft. No funding round is currently open — this exists so a real conversation has something concrete to start from, not as an active pitch.*

## The Problem
Dog toys are sold as if they're not going to be destroyed in minutes — every brand claims "durable" or "indestructible," and every dog owner knows that's not really true. Nobody in the commercial dog toy market leans into the destruction itself as the entertainment (confirmed via a direct prior-art check — no comparable product found).

## The Solution
Terrified Toys makes plush dog chew toys with a deliberately terrified facial expression — the toy "knows" what's about to happen to it — plus a small red internal-stuffing detail that shows through as it's chewed apart. The destruction is the joke, not a flaw. Three launch designs: NZ deer, rabbit, possum, $30-45 NZD retail.

## Traction (pre-revenue, pre-launch)
- Pre-launch waitlist live at [terrified-toys.vercel.app](https://terrified-toys.vercel.app) with 5 persona-specific landing pages and a working signup-to-email pipeline (MailerLite, via a custom serverless integration)
- Full brand strategy, go-to-market plan, and 8-week content calendar already built
- Manufacturer outreach sent to 3 of 8 researched suppliers for the first 25-unit production run
- "Terrified Toys" trademark confirmed clear on IPONZ (New Zealand)
- **Honest framing: zero sales to date.** This is a pre-demo-run business — traction so far is in planning, brand, and pipeline infrastructure, not revenue. Anyone evaluating this should weight it accordingly.

## Market Size
- New Zealand: ~830,000 dogs, ~465,000 registered dog owners (National Dog Database, Nov 2025)
- Australia: ~6.4 million dogs, 40-50% of households own a dog; durable dog toy retail category estimated at AUD 280-340 million in 2026, growing 5-8%/year
- Terrified Toys is a tiny, novelty-positioned slice of this — not competing on the core "durable toy" value proposition, but on the gifting/social/impulse-buy occasion (see `brand-strategy.md` personas and `go-to-market.md` for the actual targeting logic, which is narrower and more specific than "all dog owners")

## Business Model
Direct-to-consumer, social-first organic acquisition (TikTok/Instagram), no paid ad spend in Year 1. Demo run self-funded ($250 NZD). See `growth-plan.md` for the gated Year 1→3 scenario model — each stage is conditional on the one before it actually validating, not a committed roadmap.

## The Ask
**None right now.** This is bootstrapped and self-funded through the demo-run stage. If a real ask materializes later (e.g. to fund a larger production run once the demo validates demand), it would be sized against actual sell-through data from the first 25 units — not the placeholder estimates in `business-plan.md`. This document exists so that conversation, if it happens, starts from something concrete rather than a cold pitch.
