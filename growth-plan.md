# Terrified Toys — Growth Plan (Year 1-3)

**Read this as a planning scaffold, not a forecast.** There are currently zero sales, no confirmed manufacturer cost, and no manufactured product — every number below is an illustrative target conditional on the stage before it succeeding. Each year is explicitly gated on validating the one before it; this document is meant to stop the business over-committing to Year 3 ambitions before Year 1 is even proven, while still giving a real shape to plan against.

## Year 1 — NZ Launch & Validate

**Goal:** prove the core bet (a terrified-expression dog toy with a destruction-as-entertainment angle actually sells, and content about it actually spreads) before spending real money scaling it.

- **Market:** New Zealand only, direct-to-consumer.
- **Product:** 3 SKUs (NZ deer, rabbit, possum), single demo run of 25 units, second production run only if the demo sells through and content performs (per the existing 90-day KPIs in `marketing-campaign-plan.md`).
- **Revenue target:** see `business-plan.md` Financial Projections — conservative $825, base case $2,100, optimistic $8,750 (illustrative, see that doc for assumptions).
- **Unit economics:** ~$25 NZD landed cost at 10-25 unit scale (placeholder, pending real quote), retail $30-45 NZD. Margin is thin at this scale on purpose — Year 1 is about validation and content, not profit.
- **Team:** Daniel + Trinity only, no hires. Self-fulfilled (hand-packed/shipped) — see `suppliers.md` "Dropshipping Assessment" for why a 3PL isn't worth it yet.
- **Funding:** self-funded, $250 NZD demo run budget, no external funding sought or needed.
- **Gate to Year 2:** demo run sells out AND at least one of (a) a second production run gets ordered profitably, (b) organic content shows real, repeatable reach (not just one lucky video). If neither happens, Year 2 below doesn't start on schedule — revisit the whole plan instead of pushing forward on hope.

## Year 2 — Australia Expansion & 3-SKU Range Maturity (conditional on Year 1 gate)

**Goal:** prove the model travels to a second market and that the 3-design range can be produced at real volume, not just demo-batch volume.

- **Market:** add Australia as a direct-to-consumer market (shipping infrastructure for this already exists in the current tech stack — see `business-plan.md` Tech Stack & Architecture). Possibly the first limited wholesale conversations in NZ pet/gift retail (see `wholesale-strategy.md`), but DTC stays primary.
- **Product:** same 3 SKUs, but produced at a real production-run volume (100+ units per design) rather than demo-batch volume — this is the point where per-unit cost should meaningfully drop (per the 40-60% cost reduction noted in `business-plan.md` Unit Economics at MOQ 100+).
- **Illustrative revenue target:** $15,000-40,000 NZD for the year, contingent entirely on Year 1's actual conversion rate (waitlist → purchase) holding up at higher volume — this range will need replacing with real numbers once Year 1 data exists.
- **Unit economics at scale:** if landed cost drops to roughly $12-18 NZD/unit at 100+ MOQ (illustrative, needs a real quote), margin per unit improves to roughly $15-25 NZD at the existing $30-45 retail range — meaningfully healthier than Year 1's thin margin.
- **Team:** likely the first point where outsourcing fulfilment (a 3PL — Stuffed Animal Pros' "SAP 3PL" is the one concretely-researched real option, see `suppliers.md`) becomes worth it instead of hand-shipping, since AU+NZ combined volume at this stage plausibly exceeds what's manageable by hand. No full-time hires expected yet — possibly a part-time contractor for fulfilment or customer support if volume justifies it.
- **Funding:** likely still self-funded from Year 1 profit if Year 1 went well; a small funding ask becomes plausible here if a real wholesale opportunity needs upfront inventory beyond what Year 1 profit covers — this would be the first point an `investor-one-pager.md`-style pitch might actually get used for something concrete.
- **Gate to Year 3:** AU expansion proves the brand/joke travels outside the founders' home market, and at least one new SKU concept is validated as worth developing (even if not yet launched).

## Year 3 — UK/US Exploration, Licensing, Wholesale (conditional on Year 2 gate)

**Goal:** this is genuinely speculative at this distance — Year 3 should be treated as "what becomes possible if Years 1-2 go well," not a committed plan.

- **Market:** exploratory UK/US presence — likely still DTC-led (social content travels globally regardless of where the business is based, as already noted for the "Dog Content" persona in `go-to-market.md`), rather than a full UK/US operational presence requiring local entities/warehousing.
- **Product:** potential licensing conversations (e.g. a larger toy manufacturer licensing the "terrified expression" concept for adjacent products) become plausible only once the brand has real recognition — not something to pursue prematurely. New SKUs beyond the original 3 designs, informed by which Year 1-2 designs/personas actually performed (see `go-to-market.md` 30/60/90-day persona validation read).
- **Wholesale:** by Year 3, wholesale (see `wholesale-strategy.md`) could become a meaningful revenue channel alongside DTC, assuming Year 2's limited NZ wholesale conversations went well.
- **Illustrative revenue target:** wide range, **$80,000-300,000+ NZD**, reflecting how much this depends on unknowables (does a breakout viral moment happen, does any wholesale/licensing deal land, does UK/US traction materialize at all). Not meaningful enough to plan budgets against yet.
- **Team:** this is plausibly the first stage requiring an actual hire (e.g. part-time operations/fulfilment support, or a customer service role) rather than founder-only — sized against whatever Year 2 revenue actually supports, not a target headcount picked in advance.
- **Funding:** a real funding ask (or none, if organically profitable) — sized against actual Year 1-2 financials at the time, not anything in this document.

## What This Plan Deliberately Doesn't Do

- It doesn't commit to UK/US, licensing, or wholesale happening on a fixed calendar — each is conditional on the stage before it, explicitly.
- It doesn't pretend the revenue ranges are forecasts — they're scenario placeholders that should be replaced with real numbers as each year's actual data exists, the same caveat as `business-plan.md` Financial Projections.
- It doesn't plan headcount growth ahead of revenue that would fund it — every hire mentioned above is conditional on volume, not aspirational team-building.
