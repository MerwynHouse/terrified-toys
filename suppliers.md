Compiled June 2026 for a ~10-unit demo run (3 designs: NZ deer/stag, rabbit, possum — all with terrified expression + red "gore" detailing). Target: sub-$500 NZD landed cost for the demo batch, no final design files required (written brief + reference photos only).

None of these suppliers have confirmed pricing for our specific brief yet — that's what the outreach emails (see `outreach-emails.md`) are for. This doc is the shortlist + what's known/unknown about each, so outreach can be prioritized sensibly.

**Before sending outreach: see [supplier-ip-review.md](supplier-ip-review.md)** for each supplier's stated (or absent) policy on design ownership, confidentiality/NDA, exclusivity, and portfolio/marketing use — this changes the recommended contact order from a pure MOQ/price view.

## Dropshipping Assessment (researched, not pursued)

Researched whether true dropshipping (zero inventory held by us, manufacturer ships per order) is viable instead of the current pre-manufactured-batch plan. Short answer: **no — not for this product**, and the research explains why clearly enough to stop looking rather than keep searching for a better option.

### Do any of the 8 shortlisted suppliers offer dropshipping?
Checked `suppliers.md` and `supplier-ip-review.md` plus each supplier's site directly — **only one explicitly does:**
- **Szoneier** has a dedicated [`/dropshipping/`](https://szoneier.com/dropshipping/) page: *"flexible dropshipping solutions where you can place individual orders worth $1,000 or more, and they'll handle delivery directly to your end customers."* That $1,000-per-order minimum makes this a **wholesale/bulk dropship arrangement** (think: a larger reseller placing repeat bulk orders), not single-unit consumer dropshipping — it doesn't solve "ship one toy per customer order with no inventory." Worth asking directly if interested, but don't expect it to fit a single-customer-order model.
- Budsies, YourStuffMade, Stuffed Animal Pros, Child's Own, Bespoke Pet Products, Wedogy, and EverLighten: **no dropshipping or per-order fulfillment service found** on any of their sites. They are manufacture-and-ship-to-you suppliers — you receive the batch, you handle fulfillment from there.

### Print-on-demand platforms (Printful, Printify, SPOD, Gelato) — not a fit
Checked Printify's actual "custom stuffed animals" product directly: it's a **generic pre-made plush blank (bear, bunny, elephant, etc.) with a small printed fabric panel or tiny t-shirt attached** — flat printing only, explicitly recommends avoiding detailed artwork since "the t-shirts are indeed small." There is no path here to a custom-sculpted face with a panicked expression, let alone a structural red "gore" fill detail. **None of the mainstream POD platforms support fully custom 3D plush shapes** — they're built for printing a flat design onto an existing blank, which is a fundamentally different (and much simpler) manufacturing process than what this product needs.

### Dedicated custom-plush "dropshippers"
- **Fourthwall** is the closest genuine match: real custom 3D plush from original artwork (not flat printing), explicitly "no inventory required," no minimum order, ships from manufacturing/fulfillment partners in the US/UK/EU/AUS/Asia (NZ not explicitly listed). **But the catch that matters:** even though there's no MOQ, each individual customer order still goes through a full bespoke production cycle — researched lead times run **20-50 days production plus 5-8 days shipping, i.e. roughly 4-10 weeks door-to-door per order**, because a sculpted 3D plush genuinely can't be produced faster than that regardless of branding. "Dropship" here means "you don't pre-pay for inventory you might not sell," not "fast fulfillment."
- **CJdropshipping's ODM service** can build a custom SKU exclusively for your design (sample/approval process first), then hold and ship it per order from their warehouse — a viable hybrid (you don't hold stock yourself), but no confirmed per-order lead time was found, and it still requires the same sample-then-produce cycle as a normal manufacturer relationship.
- **Alibaba-listed "custom plush dropshipping"** suppliers are mostly standard manufacturers with low-by-industry-standards MOQs (50-100 units) using "dropshipping" loosely to mean "we can ship directly to your customers in bulk" — not single-unit, no-inventory fulfillment. Same category as the existing Backup/Scale-Up list below, not a new category.

### NZ/AU fulfilment (3PL) options
Found real, legitimate 3PL (third-party logistics/warehousing) providers serving NZ/AU — e.g. NPFulfilment, PACK & SEND, Fulfilment Australia, ShipBob NZ. **Important distinction: these are not manufacturers.** A 3PL only warehouses and ships pre-manufactured stock you send them — you'd still need one of the suppliers above (or Alibaba) to actually make the toys first. At 10-25 units, a 3PL is significant unnecessary overhead; this only becomes worth it once order volume is too high to hand-pack and ship from home.

### Honest assessment: dropshipping vs. the current plan
**True dropshipping isn't realistic for this product**, full stop — a custom-sculpted 3D plush with a structural gore-fill detail requires pattern-making, cutting, sewing, embroidery, and stuffing per unit. That's an inherently batch-friendly, not instant-per-order, process. Every option researched that calls itself "dropshipping" for this product category is really one of:
1. **Wholesale dropship** (Szoneier) — bulk orders shipped direct to end customers, still has a meaningful per-order minimum
2. **No-inventory-but-still-slow** (Fourthwall, CJdropshipping ODM) — you avoid holding stock, but each order takes 4-10+ weeks to produce and ship
3. **Not actually custom** (Printify and other mainstream POD) — fine for a printed flat design, useless for this specific product

**Recommendation: stay with the current plan** — pre-manufacture a batch (the demo run, then a larger validated run), self-fulfil at current scale (10-25 units is easily hand-shippable), and only reconsider a 3PL once monthly order volume genuinely outgrows what's manageable by hand (a rough threshold worth using: once fulfillment is eating meaningful founder time every week, not before). This also protects the brand's actual scarcity story ("only making 25") — a dropship model that ships in 6-10 weeks per order would work against the urgency/founding-buyer narrative already built into `pre-launch.html` and the persona landing pages.
