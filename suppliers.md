# Terrified Toys — Supplier Shortlist (Demo Run)

Compiled June 2026 for a ~10-unit demo run (3 designs: NZ deer/stag, rabbit, possum — all with a terrified expression as the main hook, plus a secondary red internal-stuffing detail). Target: sub-$500 NZD landed cost for the demo batch, no final design files required (written brief + reference photos only).

None of these suppliers have confirmed pricing for our specific brief yet — that's what the outreach emails (see `outreach-emails.md`) are for. This doc is the shortlist + what's known/unknown about each, so outreach can be prioritized sensibly.

**Before sending outreach: see [supplier-ip-review.md](supplier-ip-review.md)** for each supplier's stated (or absent) policy on design ownership, confidentiality/NDA, exclusivity, and portfolio/marketing use — this changes the recommended contact order from a pure MOQ/price view.

## Notes on Prioritization

- **Contact Budsies, YourStuffMade, and Bespoke Pet Products first** — best combination of low/no stated minimum and (for Bespoke) direct pet-toy experience.
- **Stuffed Animal Pros is not a fit for the demo run itself** (their own site redirects sub-50 orders elsewhere) — keep them on file for the second, larger production run once the joke is validated.
- **None of these have confirmed NZ/AU shipping or red-stuffing/destruction-detailing experience yet** — these are exactly the questions the outreach email asks. Treat every number in this doc as provisional until a real quote comes back.
- **EverLighten and Wedogy both have stated minimums above 10** (30 and 100 respectively) — still worth asking, since both explicitly advertise flexibility for smaller/basic orders, but don't assume they'll say yes.

## Dropshipping Assessment (researched, not pursued)

Researched whether true dropshipping (zero inventory held by us, manufacturer ships per order) is viable instead of the current pre-manufactured-batch plan. Short answer: **no — not for this product**, and the research explains why clearly enough to stop looking rather than keep searching for a better option.

### Do any of the 8 shortlisted suppliers offer dropshipping/fulfilment? (verified directly on each site — ToS, FAQ, How It Works, and any dedicated fulfilment/dropship pages, not search summaries)

**Budsies / Stuffed Animal Pros (same parent entity) — yes, a real and detailed 3PL fulfilment add-on.** Found via their own site nav ("About → Fulfillment"): [stuffedanimalpros.com/fulfillment/](https://www.stuffedanimalpros.com/fulfillment/), titled *"SAP 3PL: The 'Hands-Off' Fulfillment Solution."* Verbatim: *"Stuffed Animal Pros (SAP) provides a seamless 3PL (Third-Party Logistics) add-on for a completely hands-off production and distribution cycle. We manage the international logistics, storage, and individual fulfillment of your orders. You build the brand; we handle the boxes."* Key terms quoted directly from the page:
  - *"Direct Inbound Logistics: We land your product directly at our fulfillment center. Eliminate garage storage and local warehouse coordination."*
  - *"Shopify Collective Integration: Automated inventory sync and order routing... We bridge our warehouse to your store via Shopify Collective. When a customer buys from you, we ship automatically."* — **this is Shopify-dependent**, which matters since Terrified Toys deliberately isn't on Shopify yet (see `business-plan.md` Tech Stack & Architecture) — using this would mean adopting Shopify or finding a workaround.
  - Pricing is a separate add-on from the plush manufacturing cost itself: a one-time 3PL setup fee (covers logistics PM, customs, Shopify Collective onboarding, packing materials, *"First 3 months of storage"*), then monthly storage fees after that.
  - **Risk disclosures worth flagging:** *"Non-Payment Policy: Failure to pay results in product destruction after 90 days of delinquency."* and *"Collection Rights: For active sellers in arrears, SAP reserves the right to collect owed fees via the Shopify Collective payout split."* Exit option exists: *"Unsold product can be shipped to the client in bulk at the client's expense at any time."*
  - This is the clearest real-world example of the "3PL holds our pre-manufactured stock and ships per order" model — but it's tied to ordering production from SAP itself (their 50-unit minimum applies), and to Shopify.

**YourStuffMade — yes, also has a real "3PL and Fulfilment" service**, listed in their own site nav and at [yourstuffmade.com/pages/fulfilment-and-logistics](https://yourstuffmade.com/pages/fulfilment-and-logistics): *"Our Global 3PL (Third-Party Logistics) and Fulfillment service is designed to provide your business with reliable, scalable, and ethically managed solutions."* Notably, their team page confirms a physical Melbourne, Australia office: *"🇦🇺 Melbourne, Australia — Creative, logistics and APAC support"* — directly relevant to NZ/AU shipping confidence. **However**, no concrete minimum order size, pricing, or per-order lead time specific to the 3PL service itself was published on this page — only general manufacturing turnaround estimates (e.g. "around 8 business days" for small goods at ~100 units). Worth asking directly what the 3PL service requires at our scale.

**Szoneier — has a dropshipping page, but it's for their bags division, not plush — correcting an earlier note.** Fetched [szoneier.com/dropshipping/](https://szoneier.com/dropshipping/) directly: it's titled *"Dropshipping Bag Manufacturer | Private Label Fulfillment"* and reads *"We Help you Dropship Worldwide... With our Pro Plan or Basic Plan, you can place individual orders worth $1,000 or more, and we'll handle the delivery directly to your end customers."* The $1,000-minimum quote is verbatim and real, but the entire page is scoped to wholesale bags (their core product line) — **zero mention of plush toys anywhere on this page**, and no dropship/fulfilment language exists anywhere on their plush-specific pages (`/custom-plush-manufacturer/`, `/stuffed-animal-manufacturer/`) or homepage nav under the plush category. Treat this as **unconfirmed for plush** rather than assume it extends to their plush line — ask directly if pursuing this.

**Child's Own, Bespoke Pet Products, Wedogy, EverLighten — silent, no dropshipping/fulfilment policy found anywhere.** Checked each supplier's homepage, Terms of Service/Conditions, Privacy Policy, FAQ page, and (where one exists) How It Works page directly:
  - Child's Own: checked [Terms and Conditions](https://www.childsown.com/terms-and-conditions/), [Privacy Policy](https://www.childsown.com/privacy-policy/), [FAQs](https://www.childsown.com/faqs/), [How It Works](https://www.childsown.com/how-it-works/), and the [Bulk Order Agreement PDF](https://www.childsown.com/assets/docs/wholesale-manufacturer/customer-service-agreement-childsown.pdf) — no mention of dropshipping or fulfilment-as-a-service anywhere; the only "fulfil" hits are generic order-processing language (*"needed to fulfil custom softie ordering transactions"*).
  - Bespoke Pet Products: checked homepage nav, [Terms and Conditions](https://bespokepetproducts.com/terms-and-conditions/), and [FAQs](https://bespokepetproducts.com/faqs/) — no dropship/fulfilment mention at all.
  - Wedogy: checked homepage nav and [FAQs](https://wedogy.com/faqs/) — no dropship/fulfilment mention at all (their only legal page is generic boilerplate, see `supplier-ip-review.md`).
  - EverLighten: checked homepage nav, [Terms and Conditions](https://everlighten.com/pages/terms-and-conditions), [Privacy Policy](https://everlighten.com/pages/privacy-policy), and [FAQ](https://everlighten.com/pages/faq) — no dropship/fulfilment mention at all.
  - **Silence here is a real data point, not just "couldn't find it"** — these are functioning commercial sites with detailed policy/FAQ pages on other topics, so the absence of any fulfilment-service language suggests they genuinely don't offer one, not that it's just poorly documented.

### Print-on-demand platforms (Printful, Printify, SPOD, Gelato) — not a fit
Checked Printify's actual "custom stuffed animals" product directly: it's a **generic pre-made plush blank (bear, bunny, elephant, etc.) with a small printed fabric panel or tiny t-shirt attached** — flat printing only, explicitly recommends avoiding detailed artwork since "the t-shirts are indeed small." There is no path here to a custom-sculpted face with a panicked expression, let alone a structural red-stuffing detail. **None of the mainstream POD platforms support fully custom 3D plush shapes** — they're built for printing a flat design onto an existing blank, which is a fundamentally different (and much simpler) manufacturing process than what this product needs.

### Dedicated custom-plush "dropshippers"
- **Fourthwall** is the closest genuine match: real custom 3D plush from original artwork (not flat printing), explicitly "no inventory required," no minimum order, ships from manufacturing/fulfillment partners in the US/UK/EU/AUS/Asia (NZ not explicitly listed). **But the catch that matters:** even though there's no MOQ, each individual customer order still goes through a full bespoke production cycle — researched lead times run **20-50 days production plus 5-8 days shipping, i.e. roughly 4-10 weeks door-to-door per order**, because a sculpted 3D plush genuinely can't be produced faster than that regardless of branding. "Dropship" here means "you don't pre-pay for inventory you might not sell," not "fast fulfillment."
- **CJdropshipping's ODM service** can build a custom SKU exclusively for your design (sample/approval process first), then hold and ship it per order from their warehouse — a viable hybrid (you don't hold stock yourself), but no confirmed per-order lead time was found, and it still requires the same sample-then-produce cycle as a normal manufacturer relationship.
- **Alibaba-listed "custom plush dropshipping"** suppliers are mostly standard manufacturers with low-by-industry-standards MOQs (50-100 units) using "dropshipping" loosely to mean "we can ship directly to your customers in bulk" — not single-unit, no-inventory fulfillment. Same category as the existing Backup/Scale-Up list below, not a new category.

### NZ/AU fulfilment (3PL) options
Found real, legitimate 3PL (third-party logistics/warehousing) providers serving NZ/AU — e.g. NPFulfilment, PACK & SEND, Fulfilment Australia, ShipBob NZ. **Important distinction: these are not manufacturers.** A 3PL only warehouses and ships pre-manufactured stock you send them — you'd still need one of the suppliers above (or Alibaba) to actually make the toys first. At 10-25 units, a 3PL is significant unnecessary overhead; this only becomes worth it once order volume is too high to hand-pack and ship from home.

### Honest assessment: dropshipping vs. the current plan
**True zero-inventory, ship-in-days dropshipping isn't realistic for this product**, full stop — a custom-sculpted 3D plush with a structural red-stuffing detail requires pattern-making, cutting, sewing, embroidery, and stuffing per unit. That's an inherently batch-friendly, not instant-per-order, process. Every option researched falls into one of these buckets:
1. **Real, hands-off 3PL fulfilment exists and is well-documented** (Stuffed Animal Pros/Budsies' "SAP 3PL," YourStuffMade's "3PL and Fulfilment") — this is the legitimate version of "a partner holds our pre-manufactured stock and ships per order." But both require committing to a real production order first (SAP's 50-unit minimum applies), and SAP's specifically requires adopting Shopify (via Shopify Collective) — a real future option, not something usable at the current 10-unit demo scale or with the current Stripe-Payment-Link setup.
2. **Wholesale dropship, unconfirmed for our product** (Szoneier) — real $1,000-minimum dropship service exists, but verified directly against their own site as scoped to bags, not plush — would need to ask them directly whether it extends to custom plush at all.
3. **No-inventory-but-still-slow** (Fourthwall, CJdropshipping ODM) — you avoid holding stock, but each order takes 4-10+ weeks to produce and ship.
4. **Not actually custom** (Printify and other mainstream POD) — fine for a printed flat design, useless for this specific product.
5. **Silent** (Child's Own, Bespoke Pet Products, Wedogy, EverLighten) — no fulfilment-as-a-service offering found anywhere on their sites; they expect you to receive the batch and handle distribution yourself.

**Recommendation: stay with the current plan** — pre-manufacture a batch (the demo run, then a larger validated run), self-fulfil at current scale (10-25 units is easily hand-shippable). This also protects the brand's actual scarcity story ("only making 25") — a dropship model that ships in 6-10 weeks per order would work against the urgency/founding-buyer narrative already built into `pre-launch.html` and the persona landing pages.

**Worth revisiting later, specifically:** Stuffed Animal Pros' SAP 3PL is the one option here that's a genuinely real, well-documented, "hands-off" fulfilment service rather than a vague dropship claim — but it only makes sense once order volume clears their 50-unit minimum *and* if/when a Shopify storefront gets built (since it's Shopify Collective-dependent), neither of which applies yet. Revisit it specifically — not the general dropshipping idea — once those two conditions are closer to true.
