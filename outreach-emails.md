## Master Template

**Subject:** Custom plush dog toy quote request — small demo run (10 units, 3 designs)

> Hi [SUPPLIER CONTACT NAME / "team"],
>
> I'm Daniel, co-founder of Terrified Toys — a new pet toy brand launching out of New Zealand. I'd like to request a quote for a small demo/sample run before we commit to a larger order, and I'm hoping to work from a written brief and reference photos rather than final design files, since we're still pre-production.
>
> **Product concept:** Dog chew toys styled as terrified wild animals. Each toy's face is designed with a panicked, horror-movie expression — wide eyes, raised brows, mouth open mid-scream — because the toy "knows" it's about to be destroyed by the dog. Each toy also includes red "gore" detailing: red fabric stuffing/fill designed to spill out as the dog chews through it, simulating a (comedic, not graphic) kill. It's dark humour aimed at dog owners who'll find it funny rather than disturbing — think novelty/gag-gift plush that's also a genuinely good chew toy, not a horror prop.
>
> **Three designs, all with the same terrified expression + red gore detail:**
> 1. New Zealand deer (stag) — antlers, the "classic" startled-deer look
> 2. Rabbit — long ears, wide panicked eyes
> 3. Possum — NZ/AU possum styling
>
> **Target run:** Approximately 10 units total for this first demo/sample batch — happy to split across the three designs however works best for your minimums, or run all 10 as a single design if that's more efficient for you. Budget for this demo run is tight (sub-$500 NZD all-in), so I want to understand real costs before committing to anything larger.
>
> We do have reference photos showing the concept (terrified expression + red gore styling) that I'm happy to share — we just don't have finalized vector/print-ready design files yet.
>
> A few specific questions so I can compare options:
> 1. **Pricing:** What would unit price look like at 10 units, and separately at 100 units (for context on a future production run)?
> 2. **Working from a brief:** Can you quote and produce a sample from a written brief + reference photos, without finalized design files? If not, what exactly would you need from us first?
> 3. **Lead time:** What's the realistic lead time for a sample/demo run of this size, from brief approval to shipped product?
> 4. **Gore/destruction detailing experience:** Have you made products before with "gore" or destruction-style detailing (e.g. red fill/stuffing designed to show through torn seams), or anything comparable in the novelty/gag-gift space? Any examples would help us gauge fit.
>
> We're based in New Zealand and would need shipping to NZ (and likely Australia for a second run) — happy to hear how that works on your end too.
>
> Thanks for your time — looking forward to hearing from you.
>
> Daniel
> Terrified Toys

## Personalization Notes Per Supplier

Adjust the opening line and reference their own stated policy back at them — it signals you've actually looked at their site, not mass-emailing.

- **Budsies** — Open with: "I saw you work with no minimum order quantities and handle a lot of one-off/small custom plush projects." They're used to photo/drawing-based briefs, so question #2 should land easily.
- **YourStuffMade** — Open with: "I saw you advertise no minimum orders and a free quote/mockup process." Confirm NZ/AU shipping explicitly since it wasn't clear from their site — add a line: "Can you confirm whether you ship directly to New Zealand, and what that typically costs/takes?"
- **Stuffed Animal Pros** — Open with: "I understand your standard minimum is 50 units and you'd usually point an order this size to Budsies — I'm reaching out in case you're able to make an exception for a paid sample/demo run, or if not, any guidance on the smallest order you could realistically do would help." Frame this one as a long shot / scale-up relationship for later, not the primary ask.
- **Child's Own** — Open with: "I saw you don't have a minimum order limit and start new clients with a sample order." Good fit for question #2/#3 as written.
- **Szoneier** — Open with: "I saw you can accommodate orders from 1 to 10,000 units." Flag their stated 15-40 day sample lead time and ask directly: "I saw sample lead times of 15-40 days mentioned — does that apply to an order this size, or could it be faster for a simpler design?"
- **Bespoke Pet Products** — Open with: "I saw you specifically work with small start-up pet brands and offer full design/development for dog toys, including plush." This is the best category-fit supplier — be direct about being a brand-new, two-person startup; their positioning explicitly courts that. Also ask directly: "I noticed your published MOQs for some product lines are higher (e.g. 300 for collars) — does that apply to plush toys too, or is there flexibility for a first sample run?"
- **Wedogy** — Open with: "I saw you mention lower MOQs are possible for basic models, and that you can quote from photos or a mood board within 1-2 business days." Ask directly whether a 10-unit run is realistic given their standard 100/design MOQ, and what "basic model" means in their pricing.
- **EverLighten** — Open with: "I saw your minimum order is 30 units, which is the lowest I've found for a custom plush run." Ask directly whether they'd consider an even smaller sample run (10 units) given it's a paid sample order, not a discount-seeking bulk request.
