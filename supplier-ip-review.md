# Supplier IP & Confidentiality Review

Researched June 2026, before sending outreach emails for the demo run (see `outreach-emails.md`). For each supplier in `suppliers.md`, this covers: design ownership claims, confidentiality/NDA policy, exclusivity, and portfolio/marketing use of customer products — quoting actual policy wording where found, and flagging explicitly where a supplier is silent on a topic (silence is a real finding, not a "pass").

**Caveat on method:** several of these sites block automated fetching (Cloudflare/bot protection), so some quotes come from cached/indexed text rather than a live fetch — flagged per-supplier. Treat this as a first-pass review, not a legal opinion; nothing here replaces actually reading the live ToS yourself before signing anything or sending final artwork.

## Risk Summary

## Recommendation

**Contact in this order, from an IP-safety standpoint:** Bespoke Pet Products → Child's Own → YourStuffMade → EverLighten, then Budsies/Stuffed Animal Pros (request their named NDA explicitly if you go this route), and treat Szoneier/Wedogy as lowest priority until they answer the IP/confidentiality question directly in writing — don't assume their silence means "no policy," it just means "unverified."

Practically: the actual brief we're sending (see `outreach-emails.md`) is a written description + two reference photos, not finished commercial artwork — so the stakes here are lower than if we were sending final print-ready files. Still, worth sending the brief to the Low-risk suppliers first while the joke/designs are still easy to recreate elsewhere if something feels off.

## Per-Supplier Detail

### Bespoke Pet Products — Low Risk
Source: [Terms and Conditions](https://bespokepetproducts.com/terms-and-conditions/) (fetched directly, live).

- **Ownership:** *"1.3.1. All bespoke designs created exclusively for the client become the property of the client once paid for."*
- **No unauthorized use:** *"1.3.2. We do not reproduce, share or distribute client designs, concepts or ideas unless instructed by the client."*
- **Their own pre-existing IP stays theirs:** *"1.3.3. Internal methods, processes or pre-existing IP remain the property of Bespoke Pet Products."*
- **NDA:** *"1.9.1. Clients may provide an NDA for our review. 1.9.2. If the terms are reasonable, we will sign it. 1.9.3. Regardless of NDA status, we maintain strict confidentiality at all times."*
- **Portfolio/marketing:** *"1.10.1. Our website serves as our public portfolio containing approved work. 1.10.2. For confidentiality reasons, we do not share client-specific designs or internal project files."* — i.e. they explicitly distinguish "approved [for publication] work" from confidential client-specific work, and state the latter is not shared.
- **Confidentiality clause (general terms section):** *"12.1. Both parties must keep non-public information confidential. 12.2. This obligation continues after contract termination."*
- **Note:** a separate clause (Section 9, likely scoped to their standard/private-label product lines rather than bespoke work) states *"9.1. All IP in our standard products remains ours unless assigned"* and *"9.3. Clients grant us a licence to use branding assets to fulfil orders."* — worth asking which section applies if ordering anything off their standard catalog later.

### Child's Own — Low Risk
Source: [Bulk Order Customer Services Agreement PDF](https://www.childsown.com/assets/docs/wholesale-manufacturer/customer-service-agreement-childsown.pdf) (fetched directly, live).

- **Ownership:** *"Ownership of Intellectual Property (i) The Work Production and copyright and all Intellectual Property rights in and to such Work Product created and provided by Child's Own to the Customer pursuant to this Agreement Shall be owned by the Client."*
- **Confidentiality/NDA:** not found anywhere in this agreement or their general [Terms and Conditions](https://www.childsown.com/terms-and-conditions/) — silent, not hostile.
- **Exclusivity:** not addressed — silent.
- **Portfolio/marketing use:** not addressed in either document — silent. They do run a public [Gallery](https://www.childsown.com/gallery/) of past work, but no clause was found describing whether/how customer orders end up there or whether it's opt-in.
- **Note:** their general site Terms and Conditions (for the consumer side of the business) separately state *"You acknowledge and agree that all copyright, trademarks and all other intellectual property rights in all materials and/or content made available as part of your use of the Site shall remain at all times vested in us or our licensors"* — this is standard "the website itself" language, not about customer-submitted designs, and doesn't contradict the bulk agreement's clean ownership clause above.

### YourStuffMade — Low Risk
Source: [Terms of Service](https://yourstuffmade.com/policies/terms-of-service) (fetched directly, live).

- **Ownership:** *"Artwork, Files and Intellectual Property You keep ownership of artwork, logos, files and designs you provide to us, subject to any rights owned by other parties."*
- **Scope of license granted:** *"By sending us artwork, files, designs, logos, characters, brand assets or reference images, you give us permission to use them for quoting, proofing, sampling, manufacturing, quality control, packaging, shipping and customer support."* — notably, marketing/advertising/portfolio use is **not** in this list.
- **Confidentiality/NDA:** not found in either the Terms of Service or Privacy Policy — silent.
- **Exclusivity:** not found — silent.
- **Portfolio/marketing:** no clause found granting them rights to use customer designs/products for marketing — consistent with the narrow license above, but still worth confirming directly since absence of a "we won't" clause isn't the same as a guarantee.

### EverLighten — Low-Medium Risk
Source: [Terms and Conditions](https://everlighten.com/pages/terms-and-conditions) (fetched directly, live).

- **Website content ownership (general, not design-specific):** *"4.2 ... 4.3 Other than content and designs which you own, which you may have opted to include on this Website under these Terms, EverLighten and/or its licensors own all rights to the intellectual property and material contained in this Website, and all such rights are reserved."* — the "opted to include" phrasing implies customer designs appearing on their site/gallery is opt-in, not automatic, though the clause is generic boilerplate (reads like a standard Shopify legal template) rather than a manufacturing-specific agreement.
- **Confidentiality/NDA:** not found — silent.
- **Exclusivity:** not found — silent.
- **Portfolio/marketing:** the only relevant language is the opt-in carve-out above; no explicit clause describing how/when they'd ask before featuring a customer's manufactured product.

### Budsies — Medium Risk
Sources: [support article on copyright](https://support.budsies.com/support/solutions/articles/5000645293-do-i-retain-copyright-of-my-artwork-) (fetched directly) and the live website Terms of Service (fetched via `stuffedanimalpros.com/terms-of-service/`, which renders Budsies' own legal text — see Stuffed Animal Pros entry below for why).

- **Public-facing assurance (support article):** *"you will always retain ownership of your artwork and character(s)"* and *"Budsies does not require you to give up ownership of intellectual property rights in your images."*
- **But the actual website Terms of Service is far broader.** Under "Website Content, Ownership, Limited License, and Rights of Others," content submitted to/through the site is "User-Generated Content," and: *"by [...] submission of your User-Generated Content, you hereby grant to Budsies and our designees [...] the non-exclusive, unrestricted, unconditional, unlimited, worldwide, irrevocable, perpetual, and cost-free right and license to use, copy, record, distribute, reproduce, disclose, sell, re-sell, sublicense (through multiple levels), display, publicly perform, transmit, publish, broadcast, translate, make derivative works of, and otherwise exploit in any manner whatsoever, all or any portion of your User-Generated Content."*
- **Explicitly non-confidential:** *"Non-Confidentiality of Your User-Generated Content. [...] your User-Generated Content will be treated as non-confidential and non-proprietary and will not be returned."*
- **No special relationship by default:** *"you and Budsies do not have a confidential, fiduciary or any other special relationship by virtue of your use of the Website or your communications to Budsies through or related to the Website."*
- **Marketing use explicitly included:** the same clause permits use of "ideas, concepts, know-how, or techniques contained in any [...] Content for any purposes whatsoever, including developing, manufacturing, and marketing products and/or services," plus a right to use "your name, persona, and likeness [...] without any obligation or remuneration to you."
- **The discrepancy matters:** the consumer-friendly support article and the literal Terms of Service don't fully square with each other. The support article frames this as being about general "Website" content (reviews, contest entries, etc.) rather than the specific artwork submitted for a paid order — but the ToS language is broad enough that it's not obviously limited that way. **Recommend getting written confirmation by email** that the order-specific artwork/brief is treated as confidential and non-marketing-use before sending anything, rather than relying on the ToS or the FAQ alone.
- **Exclusivity:** a third-party search summary (not independently verified against Budsies' own primary text) suggested Budsies "will only use your character to create a stuffed animal for you" and won't sell to others without agreement — flagging this as **unverified paraphrase**, not a confirmed quote, since the live ToS itself doesn't contain that specific sentence in what was retrieved.

### Stuffed Animal Pros — Medium Risk
Source: [NDA page](https://www.stuffedanimalpros.com/nda/) and [Terms of Service](https://www.stuffedanimalpros.com/terms-of-service/) (both fetched directly, live).

- **They are the only supplier of the 8 with a dedicated, named NDA offer:** *"We take our client's confidentiality and the protection of their characters very seriously."* They state they're *"happy to send you one"* (their standard NDA), noting *"Your particular design will always be protected!"* and that they *"receive too many NDAs to review each of them individually and therefore rely on our standard NDA to protect all clients equally and fairly,"* but will use a client-specific NDA on request: *"If you require that we use your specific NDA, please send us an email."*
- **However, their general website Terms of Service is the identical Budsies legal template** (the page literally refers to "Budsies" throughout, confirming Stuffed Animal Pros and Budsies share the same parent legal entity/terms) — meaning the same broad User-Generated-Content license, "non-confidential by default," and marketing-use language quoted in the Budsies section above applies here too, **unless you specifically request and execute their NDA.**
- **Net effect:** worse than Budsies on paper (same default terms) but better in practice if you actually take them up on the NDA offer, since it's explicitly named and offered upfront rather than something you'd have to ask whether exists.
- **Also note:** Stuffed Animal Pros isn't actually a fit for this demo run regardless (50-unit minimum, see `suppliers.md`) — this entry matters more for the second/larger production run than for the immediate outreach.

### Szoneier — Unclear Risk
Sources: homepage and `/custom-plush-manufacturer/` page (fetched directly, live); no Terms of Service, Privacy Policy, or NDA document could be located anywhere on the site — the only legal page found in the footer is a [Cookies Policy](https://szoneier.com/cookies-policy).

- **Marketing claim, no backing document:** their plush manufacturer page states they offer *"strict IP protection"* for "Toy Brands & IP Owners," but this is positioning copy on a sales page, not a linked or findable policy document.
- **Confidentiality/NDA, exclusivity, ownership, portfolio use:** **no policy found on any of these** — genuinely silent, not just hard to find. Third-party industry articles mention NDAs being "available" from Chinese plush manufacturers generally, but nothing on Szoneier's own site confirms this for them specifically.
- **Recommendation if contacting them:** ask directly and in writing for their NDA template and IP ownership terms before sending the brief, since nothing is published to fall back on.

### Wedogy — Unclear Risk
Source: homepage and the only discoverable legal page, at `/privacy-policy/` (fetched directly, live) — despite the URL, the page's actual content is a generic Terms-of-Service-style template (liability caps, export-control/embargoed-country representations) with **zero mention of intellectual property, design ownership, confidentiality, or content rights of any kind.**

- This reads like an unmodified legal template (possibly a default WordPress/plugin boilerplate) rather than anything tailored to their actual manufacturing business.
- **No policy found** on any of the 5 review points — silence here is more concerning than Szoneier's, since even the page that exists doesn't address the topic at all.
- **Recommendation if contacting them:** same as Szoneier — get IP/confidentiality terms in writing directly, don't assume any protection exists by default.
