# Terrified Toys — Go-To-Market Strategy

Detailed, persona-by-persona GTM built on top of `brand-strategy.md` (who we are) and `marketing-campaign-plan.md` (tactical campaign/calendar). This doc answers: for each of the 5 MailerLite groups, specifically who they are, where to find them in NZ/AU, what message lands, what content to make, how they move from cold to paying, and what to do pre-launch / launch week / post-launch. Read this alongside `brand-strategy.md` Section 5-6 (personas + channel table) — this doc goes deeper and adds the campaign-phase tactics that doc doesn't cover.

**Honest caveat:** specific community names, Facebook groups, and influencer categories below are researched/best-judgment for the NZ/AU pet space, not verified partnerships — treat names as starting search terms, confirm relevance/activity level before investing time, and don't assume any named account or group will say yes.

---

## 1. Waiting List (general / persona unknown)

**Who they are:** Anyone who hit the generic `pre-launch.html` page without a clear persona signal yet — warm-outreach friends/family, people from a generic bio link, or early low-context traffic. NZ/AU dog owners broadly, 20-45.

**Where to reach them:** Wherever the persona isn't yet known — generic Instagram/TikTok bio link, warm DM/text outreach (`warm-outreach-dms.md`), word of mouth.

**What message lands:** The core brand pitch as-is — "it knows what's coming," dry humour, no persona-specific spin.

**What content works:** The general founder post (`instagram-posts.md`), general product/destruction content. This group is the catch-all, so keep messaging broad rather than over-fitting to one angle.

**Conversion journey:** Awareness (sees a generic post/DM/bio link) → waitlist signup on `pre-launch.html` → general warm-up emails → founding-buyer email in week 7 → Stripe Payment Link purchase.

**Pre-launch tactics:** Send `warm-outreach-dms.md` to the 10 warm buyers now; this is the fastest way to seed this group and get the first real signups/social proof (the "47 already on the list" counter becomes true rather than aspirational).

**Launch week tactics:** Founding-buyer email goes out to this whole group same as the others; this group is also where any press/word-of-mouth interest lands first.

**Post-launch tactics:** Once personas are validated, retire reliance on this group — new traffic should increasingly land on a persona-specific page instead. Keep it as the fallback default, not the primary destination.

**Partnerships/influencers:** None specific — this group doesn't have a natural community to target, by definition.

## 2. Petty Partner

**Who they are specifically (NZ/AU):** People (often 25-45, any gender) buying a gift for a partner's dog with a wink of relationship mischief. Common trigger moments: partner constantly posts about their dog on Instagram, partner's dog already has too many toys, "new puppy" gifting, or just straightforward couple banter where dragging the dog into it is the joke. Skews slightly more female-purchaser in NZ/AU gifting culture generally, but framing should stay gender-neutral.

**Where to reach them:**
- Instagram/Facebook — general feed, not niche groups; this persona isn't in a "dog community," they're in their normal social feed
- Gift-occasion search/browse moments: "funny gift for boyfriend's dog," "weird dog gift NZ" type searches
- Couple-humour meme accounts and relationship-banter content (NZ/AU meme pages, e.g. accounts in the "couples being couples" or regional meme-page space) — good for boosted/shared reach, not necessarily paid partnership
- Their own partner's dog-focused Instagram — if the dog has any social presence, that's the actual discovery surface (this persona follows the dog account, not pet brand accounts)

**What message lands:** Relationship in-joke framing, not dog-ownership framing. "I saw this and thought of you (and your dog)" energy. The joke is about the *relationship dynamic*, not the toy's mechanics.

**What content works:** Couple-banter format — short relationship-skit-style video or a simple "giving my partner's dog a toy that knows its fate" caption format. Carousel/static posts work better here than fast-cut destruction video, since the entry point is the gifting moment, not the destruction itself.

**Conversion journey:** Sees relationship-banter content in normal feed → curious/amused → lands on `landing/petty-partner.html` → signs up to Petty Partner MailerLite group → warm-up email reinforces the "plausible deniability" gift angle → founding-buyer email converts at the gifting moment (birthday/anniversary/just-because).

**Pre-launch tactics:** Seed the relationship-banter framing in any personal posts Daniel/Trinity make about their own relationship and dog — authentic, not staged.

**Launch week tactics:** Push gift-occasion timing — if launch week lines up with any near-term occasion (birthday, anniversary season), lead with that explicitly in copy.

**Post-launch tactics:** This persona is well-suited to evergreen, year-round low-effort posting (it's not seasonal like Prank Gifter) — keep a steady drip of relationship-banter content rather than a single push.

**Partnerships/influencers:** Couple/relationship-comedy content creators (NZ/AU-based if findable) are a better fit than dog-specific influencers for this persona — their audience is already primed for relationship-in-joke humour. Research specific accounts before reaching out; don't assume size correlates with fit.

## 3. Hunting Lad

**Who they are specifically (NZ/AU):** Owns a working or hunting dog (lab, pointer, spaniel, similar), embedded in NZ/AU outdoors/hunting culture. Possum is a genuinely strong cultural hook here — possums are a notorious NZ pest, and "good dog, get the possum" is a real, common bit of culture, not an invented joke. Skews male, 20-45, dry rather than loud humour, skeptical of gimmicks.

**Where to reach them:**
- Facebook groups: NZ hunting and outdoors communities, gun-dog/working-dog breed groups, possum-control/pest-culling community pages (search terms: "NZ hunting dogs," "gun dog NZ," "deer hunting NZ," "possum control NZ" — these communities are active on Facebook more than Instagram/TikTok)
- Instagram: hunting/outdoors lifestyle accounts and gun-dog training accounts
- Rural/outdoors retailer audiences (Hunting & Fishing NZ, Trapper Bob, etc. — not for paid partnership necessarily, but their audience overlaps heavily)

**What message lands:** Durability-first, dry humour, "your dog's already destroyed everything else" angle. The possum design specifically can carry a "now it can finally get a possum, sort of" cultural joke that's NZ-native and won't translate everywhere — that's fine, it's not supposed to.

**What content works:** Straightforward demo footage — the toy actually surviving real chewing before it goes, filmed plainly, not over-produced. This audience distrusts polish; raw, real footage with a dry caption outperforms anything that looks like a staged ad.

**Conversion journey:** Sees a no-nonsense demo/durability post in a hunting/dog-breed Facebook group → lands on `landing/hunting-lad.html` (durability-forward copy) → signs up to Hunting Lad group → warm-up email reinforces "still a real toy, we checked" → founding-buyer email closes with the same durability framing.

**Pre-launch tactics:** Identify and join 3-5 relevant NZ hunting/gun-dog Facebook groups now (as a member, not posting yet) — understand group norms before posting anything promotional; most of these groups are wary of brands dropping in.

**Launch week tactics:** Share in those groups once stock/real footage exists — lead with the dog's reaction and durability, not the brand pitch; mention NZ-made/NZ-founded explicitly, this audience favours local.

**Post-launch tactics:** If the possum design lands well here, consider a possum-specific post/seasonal tie-in (possum control season framing) as a recurring content angle distinct from the general brand calendar.

**Partnerships/influencers:** Working-dog trainers, gun-dog breeders, or NZ outdoors/hunting content creators with real dogs in their content are the best fit — look for smaller, genuinely engaged accounts over large generic "hunting" pages. Avoid anything that reads as a lifestyle/fashion hunting account rather than a real-dog-owner account.

## 4. Dog Content

**Who they are specifically (NZ/AU + global):** Heavy TikTok/Reels users embedded in dog-meme and destruction-compilation content ecosystems. Skews younger, 20-32. This is the only persona where geography matters less — dog-destruction content travels globally, so while the brand and shipping are NZ/AU-first, discovery isn't geo-limited the way the other personas are.

**Where to reach them:**
- TikTok FYP — dog destruction/reaction compilation content, dog-meme audio trends, "POV: my dog and a new toy" format videos
- Instagram Reels — same content repurposed
- Existing large dog-content/meme accounts as a reference point for tone and format (e.g. globally known dog-comedy accounts) — not necessarily outreach targets, but useful to study for what's currently working in this content category before posting

**What message lands:** No message — the video is the message. Caption plays it completely straight, minimal copy, let the destruction speak.

**What content works:** Fast-cut or slow-mo destruction footage, trending-audio-compatible, no voiceover/explanation needed. This is the format most likely to actually go viral, so production should prioritize capturing a genuinely good "reaction moment" over polish.

**Conversion journey:** Scrolls FYP → video stops the scroll → bio link → `landing/dog-content.html` (minimal copy, visual-first) → signs up to Dog Content group → warm-up email is just as minimal ("it always loses") → founding-buyer email, same minimal tone.

**Pre-launch tactics:** This persona can't really be served until there's real footage — prioritize getting the warm pre-sell buyers' dogs filmed as early as possible (week 5-6 per the campaign calendar) since this persona's entire funnel depends on having actual destruction content, not renders or descriptions.

**Launch week tactics:** Post at high frequency during the week stock arrives — this persona rewards volume and trend-following more than the other four; consider 1 piece of content per day during the unboxing/reaction week rather than spacing it out.

**Post-launch tactics:** Keep producing destruction content indefinitely — this persona/content type isn't a one-off campaign beat, it's the ongoing organic engine described in `marketing-campaign-plan.md`.

**Partnerships/influencers:** Small-to-mid dog-content creators (the "my dog destroys everything" content niche specifically, not general pet influencers) are worth seeding free product to once stock exists — look for creators whose existing content already centers on destruction/reaction moments, since that's a closer fit than general "cute dog" accounts.

## 5. Prank Gifter

**Who they are specifically (NZ/AU):** Buying for white elephant, Secret Santa, or group gift exchanges — doesn't need to own a dog. Motivated by social status within a friend group ("funniest gift in the room"). Strongly seasonal.

**Where to reach them:**
- Instagram/Facebook gift-search-intent content — posts framed explicitly around "best white elephant gift" / "secret santa ideas NZ"
- Workplace/friend-group gift-exchange culture content (common in NZ/AU office Christmas culture specifically)
- Gift-guide style content from meme pages or gifting-focused accounts around Nov-Dec

**What message lands:** Fast visual read, gifting-occasion-first framing. This persona isn't browsing dog products, so the hook has to work even if they have no dog-ownership context at all.

**What content works:** Carousel/static "gift guide" style posts, punchy single-line captions, strong visual-first presentation since there's no time to build context.

**Conversion journey:** Sees a gift-guide-style post in the lead-up to a gifting occasion → lands on `landing/prank-gifter.html` → signs up to Prank Gifter group → warm-up email ("stock up while you can," multi-unit framing) → founding-buyer email with explicit urgency tied to the actual gifting date.

**Pre-launch tactics:** Map actual NZ/AU gifting calendar dates (Christmas/white elephant season is the obvious one; also consider "Secret Santa" workplace culture timing, which often starts planning weeks ahead) — this persona's entire funnel should be timed against real dates, not the generic 8-week campaign clock.

**Launch week tactics:** If launch week doesn't land near a gifting occasion, this persona will underperform regardless of content quality — consider whether to soft-launch this persona's content closer to the next real gifting window rather than forcing it into week 7-8.

**Post-launch tactics:** Build a recurring seasonal content calendar specifically for this persona (Christmas, Easter Secret Santa-style exchanges if relevant in NZ/AU workplace culture, "new puppy" gifting spikes) rather than treating it as always-on like Dog Content.

**Partnerships/influencers:** Gift-guide content creators and general lifestyle meme pages around the NZ/AU Christmas season are a better fit than dog-specific influencers — this persona is reached through gifting culture, not pet culture.

## Overarching GTM Timeline (ties all 5 together)

| Phase | Weeks | What happens across all 5 personas |
|---|---|---|
| **Pre-launch** | 1-4 | Waitlist pages live (done). Warm outreach DMs sent to 10 buyers (Waiting List seed). Petty Partner/Hunting Lad content can start now using existing reference photos + relationship/durability framing — doesn't require final product photos. Dog Content and Prank Gifter are largely blocked until real footage/seasonal timing exist, so lower activity here for now. Join relevant Hunting Lad Facebook groups as a member. Map Prank Gifter's real gifting-calendar dates. |
| **Demo arrival** | 5-6 | Warm buyers receive toys, get asked to film reactions — this is the single highest-leverage moment for Dog Content persona content. Unboxing video. First real product photography replaces the placeholder reference images across all persona pages. |
| **Launch week** | 7 | Founding-buyer email fires to all 5 MailerLite groups simultaneously (persona-specific copy, same offer). Cold-audience ordering opens. Dog Content persona gets highest posting frequency this week. Petty Partner/Hunting Lad get steady, evergreen posting. Prank Gifter only gets a full push if launch week lines up with a real gifting occasion — otherwise soft-launch and wait. |

Prank Gifter shifts to seasonal-calendar-driven content rather than continuous push. Reassess which of the 2 "proposed" personas (Dog Content, Prank Gifter — see `brand-strategy.md` Section 5) actually converted, and whether to formally validate, adjust, or retire either. |

## What "Success" Looks Like

**30 days (roughly through demo arrival/launch week):**
- 10/10 warm pre-sell units sold
- All 5 MailerLite groups have non-zero signups (Waiting List should lead; Dog Content and Prank Gifter may lag until real content/seasonal timing exist — that's expected, not a failure signal yet)
- Real product photography/footage exists, replacing the two placeholder reference images everywhere
- At least one piece of warm-buyer reaction content collected

**60 days (roughly through first month post-launch):**
- 75-100 total waitlist signups across all 5 groups (per existing KPI in `marketing-campaign-plan.md`)
- Founding-buyer email sent and converting at a measurable rate per persona group — this is the first real signal on which personas actually convert, not just sign up
- At least one Dog Content piece showing real organic reach (the "breakout video" goal)
- 20+ cold-audience orders (40% of the 50-order, 90-day target)

**90 days:**
- 50 cold-audience orders total
- Clear read on which of the 5 personas over/under-performed relative to signup volume — this is the data point that should drive whether Dog Content/Prank Gifter get validated as permanent personas or folded back into the general Waiting List approach
- Enough organic content volume and real customer reactions to make a go/no-go call on a second, larger production run
