## Your Rights Under the Consumer Guarantees Act

Every product we sell comes with automatic guarantees under the CGA — these apply regardless of anything else in this policy, and we cannot and do not try to override them:
- The product must be of **acceptable quality** — durable, safe, free from defects, and fit for its normal purpose (a dog toy that falls apart in the box, rather than from normal/expected chewing, fails this).
- The product must **match its description** — including the design, materials, and the red internal-stuffing detail described on the product page.
- You're entitled to a **repair, replacement, or refund** if something's wrong:
  - **Minor problems** (something small that can be fixed): we can choose to repair, replace, or refund.
  - **Significant problems** (the fault is serious, can't be fixed in reasonable time, or you wouldn't have bought it knowing about the fault): **you choose** — a replacement of the same type, a full refund, or compensation for any drop in value.

**Important distinction for this product specifically:** dog toys are designed to be destroyed through normal play and chewing — that's the entire point of Terrified Toys. **Normal wear and chewing damage is not a fault** and isn't covered by these guarantees (a dog toy a dog has happily destroyed has done its job, not failed). What *is* covered: arriving damaged in transit, a manufacturing defect (e.g. seams failing immediately, not as part of play), or the product not matching what was described/shown.

## Pre-Order / Founding-Buyer Specific Terms

Since the first run is a pre-order (see `terms-of-service.md`):
- If we can't fulfil your order at all (production falls through, the run doesn't happen), you get a full refund — no need to ask, we'll proactively process this.
- If your specific order is faulty or damaged on arrival, normal CGA rights apply as above.
- **Change of mind is not covered** by the CGA for a normal purchase, and we don't offer a separate change-of-mind policy for the founding-buyer run, given the genuinely limited 25-unit stock — once you've committed to a unit in this small first run, we're not able to easily resell it to someone else on the waitlist if you change your mind. ## What We Can't Do

We can't put up a "no refunds" sign or bury a CGA-overriding clause in fine print — under the Fair Trading Act, that kind of term is meaningless even if we tried it, and we're not trying it. If anything in marketing or on the site ever conflicts with this policy, this policy (and your underlying legal rights) wins.
