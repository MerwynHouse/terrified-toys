# Terrified Toys — Brand Strategy

This is the foundational brand document. `business-plan.md` and `marketing-campaign-plan.md` defer to this for tone, voice, audience, and visual identity rather than redefining them — if something here changes, update it here first, then the other docs link to it.

## 1. Brand Pillars

Every piece of content, copy, or design should trace back to one of these:

1. **The Joke Is Real** — the terror is played completely straight. The toy is not winking at the camera. We never explain the joke; the face does it.
2. **Unapologetically Naughty** — sassy, a little mischievous, comfortable being divisive. We know this won't land with everyone, and that's fine — trying to please everyone makes it less funny to the people it's actually for.
3. **Let The Dogs Sell It** — real pet reactions and user-generated content carry the proof and the laugh, not a founder-origin story. "Watch this dog destroy it" outsells "two people built this in their garage." Founder authenticity is a nice-to-have footnote, not the lead.
4. **Genuinely Good, Not Just A Gag** — it's a real, durable plush. The destruction is earned, not instant. People rebuy because their pet actually loved it, not just for the joke.

## 2. Brand Personality & Voice

**Personality in one line:** sassy, dry, a little feral — the toy knows its fate and so do we.

**Tone attributes:** funny, quirky, naughty, deadpan, warm underneath the edge. Not corporate-cute, not try-hard-edgy. Confident enough to let a joke sit without over-explaining it.

**Do:**
- Play the toy's terror completely straight — the comedy is in the deadpan delivery, not exclamation points.
- Let video/photos carry the punchline; captions support, they don't explain.
- Be a little smug about the destruction ("called it").
- Write like a person texting a friend, not a brand posting an ad.

**Don't:**
- Don't wink at the camera or break character to explain the joke.
- Don't soften copy to avoid mildly offending someone — a few raised eyebrows is the tone working as intended.
- Don't lead with founder/origin-story copy — UGC and product-in-action come first.
- Don't use exclamation-point enthusiasm or generic DTC-brand cheerfulness ("We're SO excited!!").

**Example lines (in-voice):**
- CTA: "Reserve Mine — My Dog Doesn't Need To Know Yet"
- CTA: "Get In Before It Knows You're Coming"
- Email subject: "It's heard about you."
- Caption: "Day 1 of giving him a toy that knows its fate."
- Caption: "She didn't ask for this. He bought it anyway."
- Product copy: "Survived approximately 4 minutes."

## 3. Visual Identity

Formalized from what's already live on `pre-launch.html` — this is the locked identity, not a placeholder.

**Palette:**
| Name | Hex | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| Void (background) | `#0d0d0e` | Primary background |
| Panel (soft background) | `#161616` | Alternating section background |
| Terror Red (accent) | `#e8392f` | CTAs, urgency, accent text only — never body copy |
| Terror Red (dark) | `#b9261d` | Hover/active states |
| Bone (text) | `#f4ead8` | Primary text on dark background |
| Ash (muted text) | `#9a9a9a` | Secondary/supporting text |

**Typography:** current pages use a system sans-serif fallback stack only (`-apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, "Segoe UI", Helvetica, Arial`). This stays as the body font (clean, fast, no external dependency). Headlines get a single added display font for character — a bold, slightly condensed sans (e.g. via one lightweight Google Fonts import) reserved for `h1`/`h2` only, so the page stays fast and self-contained if the font fails to load (system sans is the fallback either way).

**Photography style:** raw, real pet photos — not stock, not overly staged. The mess and motion blur is part of the brand, not a flaw to retouch out.

**Recurring visual motif:** the "It knew" rip-tag badge (small rotated label on product photos) — reuse this across persona pages and future product photography as a consistent brand element.

**Logo/lockup (interim standard):** wordmark "Terrified **Toys**" — "Toys" in Terror Red, "Terrified" in Bone, uppercase, bold, tight letter-spacing. No icon/symbol yet; revisit once there's budget for a proper mark.

## 4. Target Personas

Two are locked from Daniel's direction, two are proposed and should be validated (or cut) once reviewed.

### Locked

**The Petty Partner** — buys the toy as a gift for a partner's dog, with a wink of relationship mischief ("this'll annoy them, perfect"). Not necessarily a dog person themselves — the appeal is the bit and the banter, not the toy. Skews 25-45, any gender. Trigger: relationship in-jokes, "I saw this and thought of you (and your dog)." Found via: Instagram/Facebook, often around gift-occasion timing (birthdays, anniversaries, "just because" pettiness). Objection to pre-empt: "is this actually a good toy or just a prank prop?" — answer with the durability proof point.

**The Hunting Lad** — owns a working/hunting dog (lab, pointer, similar), outdoors/blokey culture, dry rather than loud humor. The dog is part of his identity and social content. Skews male, 20-45. Trigger: "my dog's already destroyed everything else I've thrown at it, this one at least knows it's coming." Found via: Facebook hunting/dog-breed groups, some Instagram. Objection to pre-empt: durability/quality — this audience is skeptical of gimmick toys; the "genuinely good plush" pillar matters most here.

### Proposed (validate before building out)

**The Dark-Humor Dog-Content Degenerate** — heavy TikTok/Reels user embedded in dog-meme and destruction-compilation content. Shares for the bit, buys impulsively off a single video, may not have planned to buy anything. Skews younger, 20-32. Trigger: a single piece of content landing on their feed — pure impulse, near-zero consideration time. Found via: TikTok FYP, Reels. This is the persona most likely to produce the "one breakout video" goal already in the campaign plan — highest viral upside, lower guaranteed spend per person.

**The Prank Gifter** — buys for white elephant, Secret Santa, or group gift exchanges. Doesn't need to own a dog at all — buying for someone else's, or for the joke itself. Motivated by "funniest gift in the group" status. Seasonal spikes: Christmas, white elephant season, "new puppy" gifting. Found via: Instagram/Facebook gift-search intent ("funny gift for dog owner"). Objection to pre-empt: needs the gifting angle to be obvious and fast to grasp from a single image/caption, since this buyer isn't browsing the dog-product category by default.

## 7. Per-Persona Funnels & Landing Pages

Each persona gets a dedicated landing page rather than all channels pointing at one generic page:

- `landing/petty-partner.html`, `landing/hunting-lad.html`, `landing/dog-content.html`, `landing/prank-gifter.html` — same visual system as `pre-launch.html`, persona-specific hero headline, image/caption selection, and CTA copy drawn from the table above.
- **Funnel:** persona's primary channel → their dedicated landing page → MailerLite signup tagged into a persona-specific group (so the email warm-up sequence can speak to that persona specifically) → shared Stripe Payment Link at checkout (checkout itself doesn't fragment, only top-of-funnel messaging does).
- `pre-launch.html` remains the generic/default page for situations where persona isn't known (e.g. warm-buyer outreach, a general bio link).
